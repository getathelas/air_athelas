The **Multimodal Pain Control** section in the **Visit Note** (Chart Note) helps you document whether multimodal pain management methods were used during a patient encounter and ensures accurate performance measure reporting.

This documentation directly impacts quality measures and compliance reporting. When you mark whether multimodal pain management methods were used, the system automatically determines the performance outcome: **PERFORMANCE MET**, **EXCEPTIONS**, or **PERFORMANCE NOT MET**.

## Where to Find Multimodal Pain Control

The **Multimodal Pain Control** section is located within the **Assessment** section of your **Visit Note** (Chart Note).

**To access it:**

1. Open your **Calendar** from the left navigation menu
2. Select the relevant **Appointment**
3. Ensure the appointment is checked in
4. Click **View Note** to open the Chart Note
5. Navigate to the **Assessment** section
6. Find the **Multimodal Pain Control** subsection

**Note:** If you don't see the **Multimodal Pain Control** section in your Chart Note, you may need to add it to your Appointment Type template. Go to **Preferences → Appointment Types**, select or create an appointment type, and click **+ Add Section** to add the Multimodal Pain Control section to the Assessment.

<Info>
For more details on opening Chart Notes, see the [Navigate your Chart Note](/air_provider/fill_a_chart_note/nav_chart_note) guide. To learn about adding sections to Chart Notes, review [Other Sections within the Chart Note](/air_provider/fill_a_chart_note/other_sec_chart_note).
</Info>

## How to Document and How Outcomes Are Determined

Follow these steps to document multimodal pain management and understand how the system determines performance outcomes:

**1. Navigate to the Multimodal Pain Control section**

Open your **Visit Note** (Chart Note) for the patient encounter and go to **Assessment → Multimodal Pain Control**.

**2. If multimodal pain management methods were used**

Mark **Yes** in the Multimodal Pain Control section.

**Outcome:** **PERFORMANCE MET**

**3. If multimodal pain management methods were NOT used**

Mark **No** and select a reason from the dropdown menu.

- If the reason selected is **Emergency/Urgent Surgery** or **Medical Reason**, the outcome will be: **EXCEPTIONS**
- If you select any other reason (or no specific reason), the outcome will be: **PERFORMANCE NOT MET**

### Performance Outcome Summary

The following table summarizes how your documentation choices determine the performance measure outcome:

| **Your Action** | **Outcome** |
| :-------------- | :---------- |
| Multimodal pain management methods **used** → mark **Yes** | **PERFORMANCE MET** |
| **Not used** → mark **No** + reason **Emergency/Urgent Surgery** or **Medical Reason** | **EXCEPTIONS** |
| **Not used** → mark **No** + any other reason (or none) | **PERFORMANCE NOT MET** |

<Tip>
Always select the most accurate reason when marking **No**. Selecting **Emergency/Urgent Surgery** or **Medical Reason** ensures the measure is appropriately categorized as an exception rather than a performance gap.
</Tip>

## What This Means for Your Practice

**Accurate documentation helps your practice:**

- **Track quality measures** - Multimodal pain control is a key quality metric for many healthcare organizations
- **Maintain compliance** - Proper documentation ensures you meet reporting requirements for quality programs
- **Identify improvement opportunities** - Tracking when and why multimodal pain management is or isn't used helps identify patterns and areas for clinical protocol improvement
- **Support appropriate exceptions** - Emergency situations and valid medical reasons are automatically categorized as exceptions, ensuring fair performance evaluation

## FAQ

<Accordion title="What counts as multimodal pain management?">
Multimodal pain management refers to the use of multiple pain control methods or medications from different drug classes to manage patient pain. This typically includes a combination of approaches such as:

- Non-opioid analgesics (e.g., acetaminophen, NSAIDs)
- Regional anesthesia or nerve blocks
- Non-pharmacologic methods (e.g., ice, compression, physical therapy modalities)
- Opioid medications (when appropriate)

Consult your organization's clinical guidelines for specific definitions and protocols related to multimodal pain management.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Where do I add the Multimodal Pain Control section if I don't see it in my Chart Note?">
If the **Multimodal Pain Control** section doesn't appear in your Chart Note:

1. Go to **Preferences** in the left navigation menu
2. Select **Appointment Types**
3. Choose the appointment type you want to update (or create a new one)
4. Click **+ Add Section**
5. Select **Multimodal Pain Control** from the dropdown menu
6. Save your changes

The section will now appear in Chart Notes for appointments using that appointment type.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="When is the measure an exception vs. performance not met?">
The measure is categorized as an **EXCEPTION** when you mark **No** (multimodal pain management methods were not used) and select one of these specific reasons:

- **Emergency/Urgent Surgery** - The urgent nature of the procedure prevented standard multimodal pain protocols
- **Medical Reason** - A valid medical contraindication existed (e.g., patient allergies, drug interactions, specific medical conditions)

If you mark **No** and select any other reason or don't specify a reason, the outcome will be **PERFORMANCE NOT MET**. This distinction ensures that legitimate clinical exceptions are differentiated from opportunities for practice improvement.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How does this documentation affect reporting and quality measures?">
Your documentation of multimodal pain control directly feeds into quality measure reports that may be used for:

- Internal quality improvement initiatives
- Payer quality reporting programs (e.g., MIPS, value-based contracts)
- Organizational performance dashboards
- Compliance audits

**PERFORMANCE MET** cases demonstrate adherence to quality standards. **EXCEPTIONS** are typically excluded from performance calculations, recognizing situations where the standard couldn't reasonably apply. **PERFORMANCE NOT MET** cases may indicate opportunities for clinical protocol improvement or additional staff education.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I edit the Multimodal Pain Control documentation after submitting the Chart Note?">
Once a Chart Note is finalized and submitted, editing capabilities depend on your organization's policies and the note's current status in the billing workflow. In general:

- **Before submission:** You can freely edit all sections of the Chart Note, including Multimodal Pain Control documentation
- **After submission:** Contact your billing or compliance team to understand your organization's amendment process for finalized documentation

Always ensure your documentation is accurate before finalizing the Chart Note to avoid the need for amendments.
</Accordion>
