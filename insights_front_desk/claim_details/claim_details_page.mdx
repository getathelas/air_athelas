The Claims Page is where you manage your billing workflow in Insights. It centralizes claim review, validation, assignment, and submission in one interface, helping you process claims faster and catch errors before they become rejections.

<Info>
Rolling out February 2nd, 2026. All existing comments and encounters from Encounter Details will be available from day one.
</Info>

## What is the Claims Page?

The Claims Page gives you a unified view of all claims requiring review or action. You can filter claims by status, insurance, or age, assign work to team members, review AI-suggested corrections, and submit claims—all without switching between tools.

**Key capabilities:**

- **Filter and save views** to focus on specific claim types, providers, or deadlines
- **Assign claims** individually or in bulk to balance team workload  
- **Review AI validation** that flags errors and suggests corrections before submission
- **Collaborate with your team** through comments and activity tracking
- **Submit in any format** after reviewing as standard view or PDF (CMS-1500, UB-04, ADA)

## Getting Started

### Accessing the Claims Page

Log in to Insights at [**insights.athelas.com**](https://insights.athelas.com) and navigate to **Claims** from the main menu. The page opens with your most recent view, or a default table showing all active claims.

<Danger>
✨**Smart Tip:** Press **Command-K** (Mac) or **Ctrl-K** (Windows) anywhere in Insights to quickly search for specific claims, patients, or navigate to different pages.
</Danger>

When you first access the page, you'll see a table of claims with columns for patient name, date of service, insurance, claim amount, status, and assigned team member. Click any claim to open the detail view.

## Working with Claims

### Organizing Your Workflow with Filters

The filter bar at the top of the table lets you narrow your view by any combination of attributes: encounter status, insurance provider, claim amount, age, assigned team member, provider, or date range.

**Creating custom views:**

1. Apply filters that match your workflow (e.g., filter for claims older than 85 days with status "Ready to Submit")
2. Sort by the column that matters most (typically date of service for timely filing)
3. Click **Save View** and name it (e.g., "Urgent - Timely Filing")
4. Your view saves automatically and appears in the view dropdown

Custom views are personal by default. Managers can create team-wide views that appear for everyone, ensuring consistent workflows across your billing team.

<Danger>
✨**Smart Tip:** Create separate views for different work modes—one for quick wins (fully validated claims), one for complex cases (flagged errors), and one for timely filing urgency.
</Danger>

### Assigning Claims to Your Team

Claims work best when someone owns them. Use assignment to designate responsibility and prevent duplicate effort.

**To assign claims:**

- **Single claim:** Click the claim row, then select a team member from the **Assigned To** dropdown in the detail view
- **Bulk assignment:** Select multiple claims using checkboxes, then click **Bulk Actions > Assign** and choose the team member

Managers can filter by assigned team member to view individual workloads and redistribute claims when needed. You can also filter to show only unassigned claims for easy morning triage.

### Reviewing Claims with AI Validation

When you open a claim, Athelas AI automatically scans for common submission errors: missing modifiers, incorrect diagnosis codes, invalid addresses, and clearinghouse rule violations.

**How validation appears:**

Issues are flagged directly on the relevant fields with a red indicator. Click any flagged field to see the specific problem and recommended fix.

AI-suggested corrections appear with a sparkles icon (✨). Click the sparkles to see:

- What changed (e.g., "Added modifier -59 to CPT 97110")
- Why it changed (e.g., "Multiple therapy units on same day require modifier -59 per Medicare guidelines")
- The option to accept or reject the change

**Note:** You control all modifications. Rejected suggestions don't apply, and the claim retains original data. AI learns from your decisions to improve future accuracy.

### Collaborating Through Comments

The activity feed on the right side of the detail view shows all team member comments, status changes, and submission history in chronological order.

**Using comments effectively:**

- Leave context for the next person who reviews the claim
- Use @mentions to notify specific team members (e.g., "@Sarah can you verify this diagnosis code?")
- Check the feed before working on a claim to see if someone already identified issues

Comments persist across sessions, so your team maintains context even when claims change hands.

### Submitting Claims

After reviewing validation and making any necessary corrections, you can submit directly from the detail view.

**Before submitting:**

1. Review the standard view to confirm all fields are correct
2. Click **Preview PDF** to see the formatted CMS-1500, UB-04, or ADA form exactly as it will submit
3. Verify patient, insurance, diagnosis, and procedure information
4. Click **Submit** when ready

The claim moves to "Submitted" status and appears in your submission history. You can track clearinghouse acceptance and eventual remittance through the claim timeline.

## What to Expect

**Fewer rejections:** AI validation catches missing information, incorrect codes, and rule violations before submission. Most practices see rejection rates drop significantly in the first month.

**Faster processing:** Bulk assignment, saved views, and AI-assisted review help you process more claims per hour. Common workflows that took 5-10 minutes per claim often drop to 2-3 minutes.

**Better workload distribution:** Assignment tracking shows who's working on what, preventing the "I thought you handled this" problem and ensuring even distribution across your team.

**Continuous improvement:** AI validation rules update based on denial patterns across all Athelas customers, so your error detection gets better over time without manual rule updates.

## Current Limitations

- **Offline access:** Requires active internet connection; no offline claim review
- **Mobile:** Optimized for desktop; mobile has limited functionality
- **Historical data:** Claims submitted before February 2026 remain in Encounter Details
- **Bulk export:** Individual claim exports work; bulk export of filtered views coming soon

## Troubleshooting

### FAQ

<Accordion title="Will my existing claim data transfer to the new Claims Page?">
Yes, all existing comments and encounters from Encounter Details will be visible on the Claims Page from day one (February 2nd, 2026). Your team won't lose any historical data or context during the transition.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I still access the old Encounter Details page?">
After the February 2nd launch, the new Claims Page will become the primary interface for claim management. Historical claims (submitted before February 2026) will remain accessible in Encounter Details, but all new claims will use the new interface.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I know which claims need my attention?">
Use the filtering system to create custom views for your workflow. Common useful views include: unassigned claims, claims with AI-flagged errors, claims approaching timely filing deadlines, and claims assigned to you. Views save automatically so you can return to them each day.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens if I reject an AI suggestion?">
You have full control over AI suggestions. If you reject a modification, the claim retains its original data. You can add a comment explaining why you rejected the suggestion to help improve AI accuracy for similar cases in the future.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can managers see all team members' assigned claims?">
Yes, managers can view individual biller queues and create custom views that show claims assigned to specific team members. This helps with workload balancing and identifying bottlenecks.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What should I do if I encounter an error or bug?">
Use the feedback button in the Insights interface to report issues directly to our support team. Include details about what you were trying to do and any error messages you received. For urgent issues affecting claim submission, contact support immediately.
</Accordion>
