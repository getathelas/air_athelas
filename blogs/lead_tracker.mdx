Healthcare practices can lose up to 75% of potential patients due to ineffective lead management. While most medical offices excel at providing great patient care, many struggle with the critical bridge between a patient inquiry and scheduling an appointment. The gap isn't in clinical expertise, it's in having the right systems to capture, track, and convert every patient that walks through your door or calls your practice. 

We built the Athelas Lead Tracker to solve this exact problem. Straightforward to set up and customizable to your workflow, Lead Tracker turns patient connections into lasting relationships and frees you from time-consuming front-office tasks.

Lead Tracker can help with:

- **Patient Pipeline Management**: Tracking and managing new leads.
- **Request / Referral Triaging**: Automatically routing referrals & requests appropriately.
- **Patient Follow-ups**: Reaching out post-visit and coordinating follow-up care.
- **Pipeline Insights & Analytics**: Analyzing trends, sources, and conversion.

Here are the types of data and information the lead tracker can capture for you:

| Term                   | Meaning                                                                                              |
| :--------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Referral type**      | Categorizes the source or reason for a lead entering your system.                                    |
| **Lead**               | Contains potential patient information and essential metadata, like referral type and current stage. |
| **Reporting Category** | Groups leads for better analysis and sharper KPI tracking.                                           |
| **Treatment Declined** | Flags leads that declined treatment for visibility                                                   |
| **Stage**              | Represents a major phase in the lead tracking lifecycle.                                             |
| **Sub-stage**          | Breaks down each stage further for granular visibility.                                              |

## Lead Tracker Feature Overviews

### Configuring the Lead Tracker

<Card title>
  Setting up Lead Tracker is straightforward. On the settings page, practices can build stages that map to their unique process, whether that’s simple intake or multistep treatment planning. Each stage can include sub-stages for more granular tracking, and colored indicators help the whole team recognize status at a glance. Details like which columns show for each stage, and how each is grouped for reporting, are easily modified so that everyone sees what they need, when they need it. And as referral types and sources evolve, they’re easy to adjust.

### Creating, Modifying, & Linking Lead Profiles

<Card title>
  Creating a new lead just takes a moment. When a new inquiry comes in, staff can immediately enter all key details, capture the referral type, assign it to the correct facility, and set the right stage for follow-up. All major actions, from assigning providers to tracking contact dates, are visible, editable, and saved for future review. Once the information is entered, the new lead appears in the system, ready to progress.

    

  And modifying leads is just as simple. If details change or new information comes in, users can easily update the record to ensure it has the latest status and information. Switching a lead’s stage automatically prompts users to confirm the right sub-stage, so the entire record is accurate. You can also link leads to new or existing patient records by going into the lead directly and assigning it to a patient.

### Marking a Lead as Treatment Declined

<Card title>
  We know that not every referral turns into a patient. Lead Tracker helps practices not only record treatment declines, but learn from them. Marking a lead as declined prompts for reasons and extra details, ensuring the outcomes are documented for review. These entries appear in a dedicated section, making it easy to see which leads didn’t convert and to potentially revisit those conversations in the future when circumstances change. Practices can also reverse this status if needed, maintaining control over the history and possibilities for re-engagement.

### Lead Tracker Reporting

<Card title>
  Performance Analysis in Lead Tracker is all about actionable insight. By grouping pipeline stages into categories like Converted or In Progress, practices can quickly view stats on new leads, conversion percentages, decline rates, and ongoing opportunities. Reports break down the numbers by facility, showing exactly where growth is happening and where improvement is needed. For deeper review or external presentations, practices can export reports as CSV files.

### Intelligently Inbound Fax (AI Fax-to-Lead)

<Card title>
  Automate fax processing with Lead Tracker’s GPT powered fax parsing. When a referral fax arrives, Lead Tracker scans it and extracts key patient data, adding new leads to the system automatically. Only faxes with all required information trigger a new entry, keeping the database clean and organized. These AI-captured leads are flagged with notes and routed to the right point in the workflow for immediate action. Instead of shuffling paper, clinics can focus on following up and converting new patients, knowing that technology is catching every opportunity behind the scenes.
