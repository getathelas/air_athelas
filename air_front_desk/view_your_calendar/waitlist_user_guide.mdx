Use the waitlist to fill last-minute openings faster by automatically offering newly available appointment times to patients who asked for earlier availability.

## <Icon icon="circle-info" iconType="duotone" color="#F9345F" size={23} /> How the waitlist works

- Patients can join the waitlist from the **Athelas Patient Portal** when they can’t find an available appointment time.
- Staff can manually add patients to the waitlist from the **Calendar**.
- When a matching appointment opens (for example, a cancellation), Air can send offers to the next eligible patient(s) based on your site’s waitlist settings.

**Note:** Waitlist offers can be configured to require approval (creating an appointment request) or to auto-schedule when the patient accepts.

## <Icon icon="calendar-circle-user" iconType="duotone" color="#F9345F" size={23} /> Join the waitlist from the Athelas Patient Portal

When a patient schedules in the portal, they typically choose a **Provider**, **Facility**, and **Appointment Type** before seeing available times.

![Patient portal scheduling filters for provider, facility, and appointment type](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_1.webp)

If there are no available times that match the patient’s selections, they can join the waitlist.

![Patient portal prompt to join the waitlist](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_2.webp)

**To join the waitlist from the portal:**

1. In the scheduling flow, **click** **Join Waitlist**.
2. Select the patient’s preferences:
   - **Facility**
   - One or more **Providers**
   - **Appointment Type**
   - Preferred time windows (or **Anytime**)
3. **Submit** the waitlist request.

![Waitlist preference selection screen in the patient portal](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_3.webp)

After submission, the patient can view and manage their waitlist entry from their appointments area (edit preferences or leave the waitlist).

![Patient portal showing an active waitlist entry with options to edit or exit](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_4.webp)

## <Icon icon="list-check" iconType="duotone" color="#F9345F" size={23} /> Add a patient to the waitlist from the Calendar

If a patient can’t use the portal (or you’re creating the entry on their behalf), you can add them from the Calendar.

**To add a patient to the waitlist:**

1. Open the **Calendar**.
2. In the lower-right corner, **select** the **Requests** tab.

![Calendar view with the Requests tab highlighted](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_5.webp)

3. Review existing entries, then **click** **Add to Waitlist**.

![Requests drawer showing the Add to Waitlist action](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_6.webp)

4. Enter the patient’s appointment preferences (for example: **Provider**, **Facility**, **Appointment Type**, and preferred times), then **save**.

![Add to Waitlist form for setting patient preferences](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_7.webp)

5. Confirm the patient appears in the list of waitlist entries.

![Waitlist entries list showing the newly added patient](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_8.webp)

From the Requests drawer you can also:

- **Edit** an existing entry.
- **Remove** an entry (using the **X**).
- Hover over the **(i)** icon to see entry details.

![Waitlist entry details shown from the information tooltip](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_9.webp)

## <Icon icon="clipboard-check" iconType="duotone" color="#F9345F" size={23} /> Add waitlist preferences via the Waitlist Preferences intake form

Your site can optionally collect preferred waitlist times through the global intake form **Waitlist Preferences**.

- When a patient submits the form, Air automatically creates (or updates) a waitlist entry using those preferences.
- You can still review and manage the entry from the Calendar **Requests** tab.

**Note:** If your site uses this intake form as an ongoing “standing preference,” consider enabling settings like retaining waitlist requests (so the patient’s preferences remain available after they accept an offer).

## <Icon icon="comment-check" iconType="duotone" color="#F9345F" size={23} /> What happens when the waitlist processes an opening

When an appointment is canceled, Air can detect that the opening matches one or more waitlist entries. You’ll see a prompt giving you options to start processing or skip waitlist offers.

![Popup prompting you to send waitlist offers or skip](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_10.webp)

If you start the waitlist, the next eligible patient(s) receive an offer (typically by text and/or email).

![Example of a waitlist offer notification message](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_11.webp)

![Example of a second waitlist offer notification message](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_12.webp)

When a patient opens the offer link, they can review the appointment details and **accept** or **decline**.

![Patient offer acceptance page with accept and reject options](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_13.webp)

If the patient doesn’t respond within the configured time window, the offer expires and the opening is offered to the next batch of eligible patients (based on your waitlist bucket size).

If a patient opens an offer link after it expires (or after someone else has already accepted), they’ll see an expiration message.

![Expired offer message shown when a waitlist offer is no longer available](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_14.webp)

If your site requires approval for appointment requests, an accepted offer becomes an appointment request in the Requests tab. Otherwise, the acceptance schedules the appointment automatically.

![Accepted offer converting into an appointment request or scheduled appointment](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_15.webp)

## <Icon icon="gear" iconType="duotone" color="#F9345F" size={23} /> Configure waitlist settings

Waitlist settings are configured in **EHR Preferences** under the **Calendar** tab.

![EHR Preferences location for Calendar waitlist settings](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_16.webp)

Common settings include:

- **Waitlist on/off**: Enable or disable waitlist creation by patients and/or staff.
- **Require approval on waitlist requests**: When enabled, promotions from the waitlist require review before they are scheduled.
- **Cancel future appointment if waitlist slot is scheduled**: If the patient already has a future appointment and accepts a waitlist offer, Air cancels the next scheduled appointment (per your configuration).
- **Minimum time to fill a newly opened slot**: Don’t send offers for last-minute openings inside your cutoff window.
- **Same-patient waitlist offer exclusion period**: Prevent a single patient from receiving too many offers in a short period.
- **Time to accept by patient**: The amount of time a patient has to accept before the next batch is offered the opening.
- **Waitlist bucket size**: How many patients receive each batch of offers.
- **Retain waitlist requests for future**: Keep the waitlist entry active after acceptance (moves the patient to the back of the list).
- **Expire previous waitlist offers**: Expire older batches when a new batch is sent (or allow offers to remain active until someone accepts).

![Waitlist preferences and configuration options in the Calendar settings](/images/air_front_desk/view_your_calendar/waitlist_user_guide/waitlist_user_guide_17.webp)

### FAQ

<Accordion title="Why can’t a patient see the option to join the waitlist?">
  The **Join Waitlist** option only appears when there are no available times that match the patient’s selected **Provider**, **Facility**, and **Appointment Type**.

  If there are available times, the patient will see appointment slots instead of a waitlist prompt.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens if multiple patients match a newly opened slot?">
  Air processes offers based on your site’s waitlist configuration:

  - **Waitlist bucket size** determines how many patients receive each batch of offers.
  - **Time to accept** determines how long each batch has to respond.
  - If no one accepts, Air continues offering the slot to the next eligible patient(s) until someone accepts or there are no matching entries left.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="When does an accepted offer become an appointment request instead of scheduling automatically?">
  If **Require approval on waitlist requests** is enabled, an accepted offer converts into an appointment request in the **Requests** tab for staff review.

  If approval is not required, the appointment is scheduled automatically when the patient accepts.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can a patient keep their preferences for future openings after accepting an offer?">
  Yes—if **Retain waitlist requests for future** is enabled, accepting an offer keeps the waitlist entry active and moves the patient to the back of the list.

  If it’s disabled, the entry is removed after acceptance and the patient would need to join the waitlist again for future openings.
</Accordion>
